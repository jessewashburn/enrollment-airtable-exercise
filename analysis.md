## Summary Observations

To address the Dean's request, enrollment and completion were reviewed across three dimensions: **cities**, **program centers**, and **individual courses**.

### City-Level Engagement

City-level analysis was used to identify where participation is most concentrated geographically and to compare completion outcomes across cities.

* **Cities by Total Enrollment** - Cities were ranked by total enrollment volume. The most engaged cities by enrollment included Baltimore, MD; San Francisco, CA; and Austin, TX.

### Program Center Engagement

Program-level analysis was used to identify where participation and follow-through are most concentrated across the organization. Given the small number of program centers, results are summarized in tabular form rather than visualized.

* **Program Centers by Total Enrollment** - BCPI recorded 28 total enrollments, compared to 23 for GovEx. This represents a modest but meaningful difference in participation volume between the two centers.

* **Program Centers by Avg Completion** - Average completion rates were similar across program centers, with BCPI averaging 89.64% completion and GovEx averaging approximately 87.96%.

### Course-Level Engagement

Enrollments were also reviewed at the individual course level to identify which offerings are driving participation and strong completion.

* **Courses by Total Enrollment** - Innovation 101 was the most enrolled course with five enrollments. Approximately ten additional courses were tied at three enrollments each, indicating relatively even interest beyond the top offering.

## Conclusion

Overall, engagement is geographically concentrated in a small number of cities, with the top three cities by total enrollment accounting for approximately 45% of overall participation. In contrast, completion rates remain consistently high across program centers and courses, indicating strong follow-through once participants enroll.
